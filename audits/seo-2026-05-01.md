# SEO Audit - portdeveloper.github.io - 2026-05-01

Scope: one-page personal developer profile plus four static article pages. Goal: rank for `port`, `portdeveloper`, `port_dev`; make articles understandable/shareable for GPT 5.5 prompting, Claude skills, SkillBench, vibe coding, and Monad DevRel authority.

## Crawlability And Indexation

### Finding 1
- Issue: The public site currently serves `robots.txt` and `sitemap.xml` as 404s.
- Impact: High.
- Evidence: `https://portdeveloper.github.io/robots.txt` and `https://portdeveloper.github.io/sitemap.xml` returned 404 on 2026-05-01. Local files were untracked before this pass: `robots.txt`, `sitemap.xml`.
- Fix: Added crawlable `robots.txt` with sitemap reference and `sitemap.xml` listing the homepage plus all four article URLs.
- Priority: 1.

### Finding 2
- Issue: Article navigation linked to `../index.html`, creating an internal duplicate of the canonical homepage URL.
- Impact: Medium.
- Evidence: `articles/*.html` header links pointed at `../index.html#about`, `../index.html#projects`, and `../index.html#writing`.
- Fix: Updated article header links to `../`, `../#about`, `../#projects`, and `../#writing` so internal links reinforce the canonical homepage.
- Priority: 2.

### Finding 3
- Issue: Homepage tab links used `href="#"`, which made section navigation less crawlable and less useful without JavaScript.
- Impact: Medium.
- Evidence: `index.html` nav links for About, Projects, and Writing used `href="#"`.
- Fix: Changed tab links to real fragment URLs: `#about`, `#projects`, and `#writing`.
- Priority: 3.

## Technical Foundations

### Finding 4
- Issue: Images and the article video did not declare intrinsic dimensions.
- Impact: Medium.
- Evidence: Article media in `articles/*.html` had no `width` or `height` attributes.
- Fix: Added width/height to all article images and the video, added lazy loading/async decoding to non-LCP images, and kept first article cover images eager with `fetchpriority="high"`.
- Priority: 2.

### Finding 5
- Issue: Mobile text width was tight on small screens because the global padding stayed at `2rem`.
- Impact: Low.
- Evidence: `style.css` used one padding value across all viewport widths.
- Fix: Added a small-screen media query that reduces body padding to `1.25rem` and wraps homepage article dates onto their own line.
- Priority: 4.

## On-Page

### Finding 6
- Issue: The live homepage title is only `port`, and article pages currently lack rendered canonical, Open Graph, Twitter Card, and JSON-LD markup on the deployed public version.
- Impact: High.
- Evidence: Public source for `https://portdeveloper.github.io/` shows `<title>port</title>` only; public source for `/articles/you-are-prompting-gpt-5-5-wrong.html` has a meta description and title but no canonical, OG/Twitter tags, or JSON-LD.
- Fix: Added self-referencing canonical tags, focused titles, terse meta descriptions, Open Graph tags, Twitter Card tags, and schema markup on the homepage and each article page.
- Priority: 1.

### Finding 7
- Issue: Homepage metadata did not explicitly connect `port`, `portdeveloper`, and `port_dev`.
- Impact: High.
- Evidence: Existing homepage copy identifies `port`; social and GitHub links expose `portdeveloper` and `port_dev`, but the title/meta/schema did not join those names.
- Fix: Updated homepage title to `port / portdeveloper / port_dev - Monad and AI coding`, added alias language to the meta description, and added `alternateName` to Person JSON-LD.
- Priority: 1.

### Finding 8
- Issue: Article pages had share metadata, but not enough platform-specific context.
- Impact: Medium.
- Evidence: Local article heads had base OG tags but no `og:site_name`, `og:image:alt`, image dimensions, `article:author`, article tags, or explicit Twitter title/description/image fields.
- Fix: Added `og:site_name`, `og:image:width`, `og:image:height`, `og:image:alt`, article author/section/tag metadata, and complete Twitter Card fields to every article.
- Priority: 2.

### Finding 9
- Issue: Homepage sections had no semantic headings, and one article used flat `h2` headings for nested subsections.
- Impact: Low.
- Evidence: `index.html` sections had lists directly under `<section>`; `articles/how-to-create-a-skill-that-works.html` used `h2` for subsections under `The Two Types of Holes` and `How I Create Skill Files`.
- Fix: Added visually hidden `h2` headings for homepage sections and changed nested skill-article subsections to `h3`.
- Priority: 4.

## Content Quality

### Finding 10
- Issue: Article discovery depended only on the homepage writing list and X links.
- Impact: Medium.
- Evidence: No RSS feed was linked from page heads before the local SEO work.
- Fix: Added `feed.xml` and linked it from the homepage and article heads with `rel="alternate"`.
- Priority: 3.

### Finding 11
- Issue: The homepage social card now renders, but it uses an existing article cover instead of a dedicated 1200x630 personal-site card.
- Impact: Low.
- Evidence: OpenGraph.xyz rendered title, description, and image for the local homepage tunnel, but warned that the current OG image is 1200x480 while 1200x630 is recommended.
- Fix: Reused an already deployed article image so social previews render immediately after the HTML deploy. Left a custom 1200x630 profile OG image as a decision item.
- Priority: 5.

## Authority Signals

### Finding 12
- Issue: Person schema was too thin for AI/search authority.
- Impact: High.
- Evidence: Local homepage JSON-LD had `name`, `url`, `sameAs`, `jobTitle`, and `worksFor`, but no aliases or topic expertise.
- Fix: Expanded Person JSON-LD with `alternateName`, `description`, Telegram in `sameAs`, and `knowsAbout` for Monad, developer tools, AI coding workflows, prompting, Claude skills, and vibe coding.
- Priority: 1.

### Finding 13
- Issue: Article schema did not fully identify the author entity or topical keywords.
- Impact: High.
- Evidence: Local article JSON-LD used a minimal author object and `BlogPosting` type.
- Fix: Switched article JSON-LD to `Article`, added URL, language, keywords, richer author `sameAs`/job/organization details, publisher, and `isPartOf` WebSite.
- Priority: 1.
